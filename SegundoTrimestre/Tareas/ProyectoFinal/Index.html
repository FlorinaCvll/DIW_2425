Hyperborea

In Greek mythology, Hyperborea was a region situated in the as yet unknown northern lands, north of Thrace. north of Thrace. Its name (from the Greek (Υπερ βορεία) Hyper Boreia ‘beyond the north’) derives precisely from the fact that the wind-god Boreas was believed to dwell in Hyperborea. from the fact that the wind-god Boreas was believed to dwell in Thrace, and the Hyperboreans, his sons, would dwell further north of this kingdom, in the country of Thrace. north of this kingdom, in the country of Hyperborea!

Hyperborea

Beyond the north, in northern lands unknown to mankind. They never found our home for it was everywhere but nowhere at the same time. We are where Boreas, god of the north wind, is. Where is he? They place us above the Arctic Circle, where the Aurora Borealis are born and they are still wrong. I am a daughter of Boreas, I am hyperborea, I know how far away our home is. And you will never find me in the North for I will follow you wherever you are. Cara mia, take me away from my home, away from the North and all that I know, but do not go where I cannot follow you.
